About ETS

ETS allows you to

  • Review labor and absences daily and weekly.
  • Quickly and easily record your labor and absences.
  • Adjust your timekeeping for a prior week.
  • Record absences up to one year in advance.
  • Create permanent and temporary work schedules.

The remaining sections of this module highlight transactions you will need to perform as a new employee, along with how to enter labor hours and sign your timekeeping entries.

For more information about these and other tasks you can perform in ETS, visit the ETS Reference Center.

Getting started

You are required to complete several activities before you begin using ETS for your daily and weekly timekeeping activities.

  1. Verify that your ETS account is active. Before you get started with ETS, verify that you are active in the system. This will ensure you get paid.
  2. Validate your baseline work schedule. Before recording time in ETS, you must establish your baseline work schedule. Your baseline work schedule includes your daily scheduled hours, shift start time, and meal start time.
  3. Enter an Activity ID. An Activity ID, sometimes referred to as a chargeline, is a series of alphanumeric codes used to identify and record costs associated with an authorized work task. You will be assigned at least one Activity ID to support your work, and you must enter an Activity ID in ETS before you can begin recording time.

If you do not update the required fields in ETS properly and in a timely fashion, your pay could be impacted.

Refer to the ETS Reference Center or the related sections in this module for instructions on how to complete these activities.

Access ETS

You can access ETS from MyBoeing.com, inside.boeing.com, and TotalAccess or TotalAccess Express.

After logging on through Web Single Sign On (WSSO) and accessing one of these pages, click the ETS icon or link as applicable. You can then begin performing activities such as recording and signing (approving) labor hours.

Validate baseline schedule

Your baseline schedule reflects your regular shift start time and the hours you work each day. Before you begin timekeeping with ETS, work with your manager to learn more about work schedule options available in your work group.

To validate your baseline schedule,

  1. Access ETS through inside.boeing.com, MyBoeing, or TotalAccess.
  2. Select Create Baseline Schedule from the Employee Menu.
  3. Update your Work Schedule using the drop-down menu. S (Standard 80-Hour) is the most common work schedule.
  4. Select a Schedule Type. If your schedule is the same week after week, select the Weekly schedule type.Â 
  5. Select the Calendar button and then an Effective Date. The Effective Date must be on a Friday.
  6. Overwrite the existing hours (to the tenths of hours) in the Daily Hours field for each day that you are changing.
  7. If necessary, select the shift from the Shift drop-down menu for each day that you are changing or use the Quick Scheduler. Note: If a category 2 work schedule is entered, the shift must be 1, 2, or 3.
  8. If necessary, overwrite the existing shift start time (HH:MM) in the Shift Start field for each day that you are changing or use the Quick Scheduler. Note: This field requires a 24-hour-clock entry.
  9. If necessary, overwrite the existing meal start time (HH:MM) in the Meal Start field for each day that you are changing or use the Quick Scheduler.
  10. Click the Save Baseline Schedule button when finished.

Remember, if you do not update the required fields in ETS properly and in a timely fashion, your pay could be impacted.

Enter an Activity ID

  1. Access ETS through inside.boeing.com, MyBoeing, or TotalAccess.
  2. Select Change Task Description from the Employee Menu.
  3. Enter a short description or title representing the type of work your Activity ID supports in the Task Description field. Note: The description cannot exceed 20 characters.
  4. Enter the Activity ID your manager or finance focal provided at orientation. If this number will be used for future overhead or training activities, retain this information for the future.
  5. Click Add Task when complete.

Once added, this task will be available in the Task Description drop-down menu on the Record Daily Time screen in ETS.

If you will be using more than one Activity ID, repeat this process for each Activity ID you need to enter.

Record daily time against an Activity ID

Most employees are responsible for entering or recording time (labor or hours worked) every day at the end of their shift. Check with your manager to validate your specific charging requirements.

To record time in ETS,

  1. Access ETS through inside.boeing.com, MyBoeing, or TotalAccess.
  2. Select Record Daily Time from the Employee Menu.
  3. On the Record Daily Time screen, enter the number of hours you worked that day in the Hours column.
  4. Select a Time Category. Examples include Regular, Approved Overtime, Sick Leave, and Vacation.
  5. Select a Task Description for the work performed.
  6. Click Record Time after completing your entries.

Sign (approve) your time for the week

You are responsible for approving (or signing) your weekly timekeeping entries in ETS at the end of your shift Thursday or the beginning of your shift Friday.

Check with your manager or finance focal to determine if you need to sign off your time on Thursdays or Fridays.

If you do not sign your time in ETS as required, you may experience a paycheck shortage, which can take up to two weeks to correct.

To sign off weekly time,

  1. Access ETS through inside.boeing.com, MyBoeing, or TotalAccess.
  2. Select Sign Weekly Time from the Employee Menu.
  3. Review the time entries that you have made. If correct, select the Sign Time and Shift button to sign your time.

ETS Reference Center

The ETS Reference Center provides access to help contacts and step-by-step instructions on how to use ETS to enter your labor and absences, and perform other timekeeping activities.

Method 1

To access this tool, log on to ETS through inside.boeing.com, MyBoeing, or TotalAccess. Then select the ETS Reference Center link at the lower right of the ETS home page. You will arrive at the ETS Reference Center Contents page, where you can select the desired help resource.

Method 2

If you have already logged on to ETS and are viewing one of the ETS transaction screens—for example, the Record Daily Time screen or Employee Information screen—select the Help & Wk Auth menu option at the top of the page. Then click the ETS Reference Center option. In this case, help information specific to the page you are viewing will appear as opposed to the ETS Reference Center Contents page.
